Frequently Asked Questions

Why is the Japanese diet so healthy?

The Japanese diet is healthy because it revolves around balance and variety. Japanese meals are packed with fresh vegetables, delicious fish and seafood, sustaining you with a nutritious and happy life.

What is the ratio of chicken, meat, and seafood used?

Modern Japan consume more meat and poultry than ever. Whereas, traditional Japanese primarily ate more fish than meat.

Food Type Traditional Japan Modern Japan
Chicken 70-80% 40-50%
Red Meat < 10% 25-30%
Seafood ~ 10% 25-30%

Can I use frozen ingredients? Or do they always have to be fresh?

Japanese cuisine is based on using seasonal ingredients. Therefore, partakers limit their use of frozen foods.

Can I use canned food?

Yes, you can.

Can I use dairy products?

In Japanese nutrition, dairy is accepted but not essential. It's seen as a complement, not a core, and is consumed in small, mindful portions — especially in contrast to Western eating habits.
