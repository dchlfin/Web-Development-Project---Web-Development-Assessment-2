Your body needs macronutrients (carbs, protein, fat) for energy and micronutrients (vitamins, minerals) for overall health. Traditional Japanese meals naturally include these through balanced ingredients.

Ichiju Sansai

Ichiju Sansai (一汁三菜) is a Japanese traditional meal consisting of rice, protein (typically fish and tofu), and vegetables. This meal provides most of the essential nutrients as it is packed with macronutrients.

How Do You Implement Ichiju Sansai?

まごわやさしい

Ma-Go-Wa-Ya-Sa-Shi-Ii

The phrase ma-go-wa-ya-sa-shii-ii (まごわやさしい) has served as a guide to encourage a more plant and seafood-based meal.
Japanese Syllable Food Type Example Nutritional Benefit
Ma (ま) Mame: Beans/Legumes Soybeans, tofu, natto Protein
Go (ご) Goma: Seeds/Nuts Sesame seeds, walnuts Protein, minerals, antioxidants
Wa (わ) Wakame: Seaweed Kombu, nori, wakame Calcium
Ya (や) Yasai: Vegetables Spinach, carrots, daikon Variety of nutrients
Sa (さ) Sakana: Fish Salmon, mackarel Protein
Shii (し) Shiitake: Mushrooms Shiitake, enoki, maitake Vitamin D
Ii (い) Imo: Potatoes Sweet potatoes, taro, potatoes Fiber, Vitamin C
